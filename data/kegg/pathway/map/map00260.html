Glycine, serine and threonine metabolism - Reference pathway
[ Pathway menu | Pathway entry | Hide description ]
Serine is derived from 3-phospho-D-glycerate, an intermediate of glycolysis [MD:M00030], and glycine is derived from serine [MD:M00031]. Threonine is an essential amino acid, which animals cannot synthesize. In bacteria and plants, threonine is derived from aspartate [MD:M00025].
